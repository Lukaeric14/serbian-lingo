# Serbian Lingo — PRD

**One-liner:** A Duolingo-style iPhone app that teaches Dominika Serbian — built by Luka, because Duolingo doesn't offer Serbian.

## Why

Dominika (Polish) is Luka's girlfriend; Luka (Serbian) is learning Polish on Duolingo and loves it. There is no Serbian course on Duolingo — or any comparable app. So we build her the missing course as a gift: same learning experience she'd get if Duolingo shipped Serbian tomorrow.

## Who it's for

Exactly one person. Dominika starts from zero Serbian. She already knows how Duolingo works, so the app should feel instantly familiar — no learning curve, no explanations needed. As a Polish speaker she'll find parts of Serbian transparent; early lessons feeling fast is fine and motivating.

## The experience

- She opens the app and sees the classic Duolingo home: a winding path of lesson bubbles, her streak flame, and today's XP.
- She taps the next lesson and gets a 2–3 minute burst of varied exercises: building translations from word tiles, matching pairs, typing answers, and picking the word she hears.
- **Every Serbian word is spoken aloud in a natural voice** — exercises auto-play their sentence, and tapping any Serbian word anywhere pronounces it. Hearing the language is a core requirement, not a nice-to-have.
- Right answers get a satisfying ding; wrong answers show the correct solution and quietly come back at the end of the lesson until she gets them right.
- Finishing a lesson earns XP with a celebration screen; the first lesson each day grows her streak.
- The sentences star her world: Dominika, Julia, Agnieszka, Wieslaw, Stefan, Olivera, Slobodan, Lidija, Biljana, Nesa, Filip.

## What v1 includes

- **Course content:** the first section of the curriculum (~9 units, beginner level), mirroring the scope and order of Duolingo's Polish course — in Serbian Latin script, taught from English.
- **All the text-based exercise types Duolingo uses:** translate with word tiles (both directions), type the translation, fill in the blank, complete the translation, pick the correct meaning, match pairs, complete the chat, and word-level listening exercises.
- **Motivation loop:** daily streak with milestone celebrations, XP with a daily goal, celebration sounds and animations.
- **Two profiles, no login:** Dominika's, plus a test profile for Luka so he never touches her streak.
- **The look:** a faithful Duolingo clone — green, winding path, chunky buttons, the whole feel.
- **Quality gate:** every unit's Serbian content is reviewed by Luka (native speaker) before Dominika ever sees it.

## What v1 leaves out (deliberately)

- Hearts/lives, leaderboards, practice mode, reminders, mistake reviews, grammar guidebooks.
- Picture exercises and speaking exercises (future scope).
- Accounts, multi-user anything, App Store distribution.

## Success looks like

- She recognizes it as "Duolingo, but Serbian" within ten seconds.
- She can do a lesson a day for weeks without hitting the end of content or a single dead end.
- The Serbian is correct and natural — zero "this sounds wrong" moments.
- The words she learns, she has *heard*, not just read.

## Constraints

- Her iPhone. No hard deadline — right beats rushed. Content can grow after launch without her reinstalling anything.
